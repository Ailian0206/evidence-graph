# Evidence Graph Design Direction

## Visual thesis

A calm research instrument: editorial typography sits over a working evidence canvas, with near-black ink, mineral white surfaces, a restrained vermilion action color, and teal verification states. The interface should feel precise and authored, not decorative or generated from a SaaS template.

## Content plan

1. Hero: Ailian, engineering direction, direct work/contact actions, and an inspectable sample evidence path.
2. Selected work: Evidence Graph first, AI Photo Studio CN second, each explained through problem, approach, and current proof.
3. Practice notes: research and engineering decisions with honest draft/research status.
4. Profile band: current transition from senior frontend work into full-stack and Agent systems.
5. Footer: email, GitHub, language, and evidence-first statement.

## Interaction thesis

1. Evidence nodes and edges react as one path on hover/focus, making the graph's meaning clearer rather than adding ambient motion.
2. A restrained scan marker moves across the sample source excerpt when motion is allowed, suggesting verification in progress.
3. Sections enter through short opacity/vertical transitions; reduced-motion users receive the complete static composition.

## Constraints

- No purple or blue gradient theme, decorative orbs, nested cards, pill collections, or oversized SaaS feature grids.
- Cards use at most 6px radius. Sections remain full-width and are separated with rules, spacing, and background bands.
- Display type is reserved for the hero. Operational labels and panels stay compact.
- Letter spacing is zero. Font size never scales directly with viewport width.
- The hero plus header leaves Selected Work visible at common mobile and desktop heights.
- All primary visuals show actual product state or working interface behavior.
